**Current level**: As of late April 2026 — Brent ~$114–115/bbl (touched $126 intraday today on US-Iran action reports), WTI ~$105–108/bbl; the 3-2-1 crack spread is **above $58/bbl** (started 2026 near $20). Diesel >$5/gal, gasoline >$4/gal. CRAK ETF has put together 14 consecutive up weeks — its longest streak since its August 2015 inception. PSX, MPC, VLO all up roughly 31–40% YTD as of mid-March; subsequent legs higher have followed each Hormuz-disruption headline. Morgan Stanley upgraded PSX to Overweight on Apr 24.

**Catalyst**: Two converging in the next 4 weeks. (1) Q1 2026 earnings for the major US refiners over the next 1–3 weeks — the prints will capture the period when cracks broke from $20 toward $40, with the most extreme spreads still ahead in Q2 guidance. MPC's Q4 2025 EPS beat by 50% on $18.65/bbl refining margins; Q1 should be materially stronger. (2) Iran ceasefire trajectory — Trump's Apr 21 extension was reported as "a few more days"; an actual durable deal that reopens Hormuz cleanly is the binary that compresses cracks. The Apr 28 Iranian proposal explicitly defers nuclear and tackles only Hormuz reopening; Trump administration early signals are skeptical.

**Setup**: US refiners are geographically insulated from a chokepoint that carries ~20% of global seaborne oil. The blockade has constrained not only crude but, critically, refined-product flow from Persian Gulf refineries that supplied Asia and Europe. Asia is now bidding for US distillate. This is a textbook "we're long the only seller" setup. Prior cycles (2022 invasion of Ukraine) saw refiner equities triple before cracks normalized; current spreads are wider, current inventories are tighter, and US export capacity is the binding constraint. Q1 will print into a tape that is already long the names — but Q2 guidance is what unlocks the next leg, and that can only be drawn down by a ceasefire.

**Bull case**:
- Cracks averaged ~$40 in Q1 versus a ~$15 long-run norm; refiner Q1 EPS likely come in 50–100% above sell-side because models have not fully marked the spread. PSX/MPC/VLO have historical operating leverage on cracks of roughly $250–400M of EBIT per $1/bbl of crack expansion at full utilization.
- Q2 will be wider still — current $58 3-2-1 vs Q1 average ~$40 is +45%. Diesel inventory in the US is at multi-year lows; ARA distillate stocks are also low. Even if Hormuz reopens politically, physical product flow restoration takes weeks.
- Capital returns (PSX has 14 straight years of dividend hikes, MPC has been buying back at ~10% of cap annualized) compound the equity return on top of the spread tailwind.

**Bear case**:
- Political binary against. A full Iran ceasefire and Hormuz reopening would compress cracks fast — gasoline crashed 11–12% intraday on the Apr 17 ceasefire announcement before the situation re-deteriorated. The thesis lives or dies on a political outcome the agent cannot forecast.
- 14 consecutive weekly gains in CRAK is a positioning red flag — speculative money is long; on any de-escalation, the unwind is reflexive.
- Government policy risk: gasoline >$4 / diesel >$5 is politically toxic in the US in an election-environment year. SPR releases, export restrictions, or windfall-tax noise are real tail risks.
- Refining is structurally a cyclical, capital-returning, no-growth business — at peak cracks the multiple compresses, not expands. Buying mid-supercycle is buying a known fade in 2027.

**Invalidation**: Any of (a) verified durable US-Iran agreement that fully reopens Hormuz to free transit (no tolls, no blockade), (b) 3-2-1 crack falls below $30/bbl on rising distillate inventory, or (c) US executive action restricting refined-product exports. Any one ends the thesis; do not re-enter on the dip.

**Key data to watch**:
- PSX, MPC, VLO Q1 earnings (rolling release in next 1–3 weeks) — focus on Q2 throughput guide and capital-return announcements
- Weekly EIA distillate inventory (Wednesdays)
- Iran negotiation headlines around the next ceasefire-extension window
- Today (Apr 30) Trump decision on rumored military briefing — was the headline that took Brent to $126 intraday before the fade

**Liquidity / risk flags**: CRAK ETF AUM is small relative to its constituents; on a violent unwind the ETF can dislocate. Single-name refiners trade with very high correlation in stress — diversification benefit within the basket is limited. Crack-spread futures themselves are thinly traded retail; this is best expressed via equity.
