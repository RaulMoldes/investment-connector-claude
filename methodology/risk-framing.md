
# Risk Framing

Every report ends with this block (adapt the specifics, but the substance is non-negotiable):

```
## Risk Framing

**This is research, not advice.** These are leads for further investigation, not recommendations to buy or sell. The author is an automated research agent with no knowledge of your portfolio, tax situation, risk tolerance, or time horizon.

**What this report is NOT**:
- Not personalized financial advice
- Not a guarantee any thesis will play out
- Not a substitute for your own due diligence
- Not adjusted for your specific circumstances

**Known limitations of this analysis**:
- Based on public information available at report date; material developments after that date are not reflected
- News-driven scanning biases toward narratives over fundamentals
- Cannot access paywalled research or proprietary data
- Cannot verify management claims or footnote disclosures
- Macro forecasts are especially unreliable; regime shifts invalidate theses frequently

**Before acting on any idea**:
1. Verify all current prices and data independently
2. Read the primary source filings / data releases
3. Consider how it fits (or doesn't) with your existing positions
4. Size any position small enough that being wrong doesn't matter
5. Define your own stop-loss or invalidation level in advance
```

## Additional rules for the agent

- If any idea has **binary risk** (biotech trial readout, FDA decision, single-event crypto token), flag this explicitly in that idea's section, not just in the general block.
- If an idea is in a **thinly traded** asset (low-cap equity, obscure crypto), add a liquidity warning.
- If an idea has **regulatory risk** as the primary thesis driver (e.g., pending legislation), note that political outcomes are outside the agent's forecasting competence.
- Never imply any of the ideas are "safe". Every idea can lose money.
- Never give a specific position size or portfolio weight. That's the user's call.

## Language to avoid

These phrases should never appear in a report:
- "You should buy..."
- "Strong buy" / "Strong sell"
- "Guaranteed" / "Can't lose" / "No-brainer"
- "Safe bet"
- Any claim of certainty about future prices

## Language to prefer

- "One setup worth investigating..."
- "If [catalyst], the asymmetric payoff would be..."
- "A possible thesis is..."
- "This is interesting but unverified; the key check would be..."
