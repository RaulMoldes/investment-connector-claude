
# Thesis Structure

Every idea MUST use this exact structure:

```
### N. [Asset/Ticker] — [One-line thesis] [Horizon]

**Current level**: [price] as of [date]

**Catalyst**: What specific event or datapoint will force a repricing? Include approximate timing.

**Setup**: The context — why now? What's the backdrop that makes this asymmetric?

**Bull case**: 2–3 falsifiable claims with the mechanism. Not "it's cheap" — rather "the company trades at 8x forward earnings vs a 5-year average of 14x, and margins are inflecting because X".

**Bear case**: The strongest counter-argument. What would a thoughtful short make? Treat this seriously — if you can't articulate a real bear case, the thesis is underdeveloped.

**Invalidation**: The specific trigger that would make you drop the idea. A price level, a datapoint, a news event. If none exists, the thesis is not falsifiable and should be dropped.

**Key data to watch**: The 1–3 upcoming data releases, earnings, or events that will confirm or deny the thesis.
```

## Quality bar

An idea passes if and only if:

- [ ] The catalyst is specific, not vague ("earnings" is not enough — "Q4 earnings on DATE where consensus is X and I expect Y because Z")
- [ ] The horizon matches the catalyst timing
- [ ] Bull and bear cases are both specific and falsifiable
- [ ] Invalidation criteria are clear and measurable
- [ ] No prices or data from memory — all current figures are web-searched

## Horizon rules

- `[Short]` — catalyst within 4 weeks; use for event-driven plays (earnings, Fed meetings, binary data)
- `[Medium]` — catalyst or thesis plays out over 1–6 months; cyclical or macro positioning
- `[Long]` — structural theme or valuation convergence expected over 6+ months

Do not label an idea `[Short]` if its catalyst is more than 4 weeks out.

## Common failure modes to avoid

1. **Narrative without catalyst** — "AI is transforming healthcare" is not a thesis.
2. **Momentum chasing** — "it's been going up" is not a reason.
3. **Valuation-only** — "it's cheap on P/E" without a catalyst means it can stay cheap indefinitely.
4. **Consensus dressed as contrarian** — if Bloomberg, Reuters, and WSJ all wrote the same take last week, it's priced in.
5. **Unfalsifiable** — if no datapoint could prove the thesis wrong, it's not an analysis.

## Tone

- Probabilistic, not certain ("likely", "could", "if X then Y")
- Concrete numbers over vibes
- Short sentences; no consulting-speak
- Honest about uncertainty
